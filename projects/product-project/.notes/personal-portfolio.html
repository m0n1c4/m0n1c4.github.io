PROJECT

Build

It is time to start looking more seriously into creating a personal portfolio site to show off our awesome work.

  • Create an html document called `index.html` and design it to your liking.
  • You may want to try a simple multi-column, container-bound layout
  • Alternately, consider building off of a slightly more complex layout. (download the library code here)
  • You can find more detailed explainations of CSS layouts here
  • You may consider linking your main page to a second page containing a short bio, and your resume created in HTML.
  • Add some links to gists containing helpful sample code for easy reference and/ or code you've written that you're particularly proud of. Add lots of comments where appropriate.
  • Consider starting a "developer blog" explaining some of the simple programming concepts you've learned. Focus on one thing per blog post. The title should be a specific question, like "How to make an unordered list in HTML", and the body of the post should answer it. A good standard length for a blog post is about two paragraphs and ten lines of code.
    • You may want to consider a library to format and provide sytax highlighting for your code samples
    • Consider recording a screencast of you writing and explaining simple coding techniques and uploading it to YouTube and embedding it in your blog
  • Begin to create some projects to show off on your portfolio site:
    • A great first project is to create a "Choose Your Own Adventure" game. Linking a series of simple HTML pages together by presenting a series of situations, and allowing your visitors to click the link of their choice to progress to the next page of the story. A simple example(use [CMD + OPTION + U] to view source), and a much more complicated JavaScript enabled one
    • You have learned all the concepts necessary to implement your own spin on Make a deal, background clicker, Mondrain painting, Bad Habit counselor, Rock Paper Scissors, Quiz App and many others!

Deploy

Get your site deployed LIVE ON THE INTERNET using Divshot. This will be an exercise in "It's not what you know, it's what you can figure out."
